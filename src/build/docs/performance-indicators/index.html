Safety Performance Indicators

Safety Performance Indicators (SPIs) are metrics used to monitor and measure the effectiveness of safety management systems.

Key Metrics​

Overview Cards​

The dashboard displays four primary metrics:

Total Hazards

  • Count of all identified hazards
  • Provides baseline for safety awareness

Mitigated Hazards

  • Number of hazards with controls in place
  • Indicates safety improvement progress
  • Shows trend compared to previous period

Open Issues

  • Active hazards requiring attention
  • Priority for immediate action
  • Tracked for reduction over time

Safety Score

  • Overall system safety rating (0-100%)
  • Composite metric based on multiple factors
  • Trend indicates improvement or degradation

Visualizations​

Incident Trends​

Line chart showing:

  • New incidents reported each month
  • Number of incidents resolved
  • Comparison to identify backlogs

Key Insights:

  • Upward incident trend may indicate emerging issues
  • Resolution rate should match or exceed incident rate
  • Seasonal patterns may reveal systemic factors

Hazards by Category​

Pie chart displaying distribution across:

  • Electrical hazards
  • Mechanical hazards
  • Software hazards
  • Environmental hazards
  • Human factors

Benefits:

  • Identify areas requiring additional focus
  • Allocate resources based on hazard distribution
  • Track category-specific trends

Safety Metrics Completion​

Bar chart showing completion rates for:

  • Equipment checks
  • Training programs
  • Risk assessments
  • Safety audits
  • Documentation

Target: All metrics should achieve ≥90% completion

Leading vs. Lagging Indicators​

Leading Indicators​

Proactive measures that predict future performance:

  • Number of safety inspections completed
  • Training completion rates
  • Near-miss reporting frequency
  • Hazard identification rate

Lagging Indicators​

Reactive measures of past performance:

  • Incident count
  • Severity of incidents
  • Days since last incident
  • Mitigation completion time

Best Practices​

Monitoring​

  • Review indicators weekly or monthly
  • Set target values for each metric
  • Track trends over time, not just snapshots
  • Compare against industry benchmarks

Action​

  • Investigate negative trends immediately
  • Celebrate positive improvements
  • Adjust safety programs based on data
  • Share results with stakeholders

Reporting​

  • Create regular safety reports
  • Use visualizations to communicate clearly
  • Include context and interpretation
  • Document corrective actions taken

Usage​

Navigate to the Dashboard and select the Safety Performance tab to:

  • View real-time safety metrics
  • Analyze trends and patterns
  • Export data for reporting
  • Identify areas for improvement
